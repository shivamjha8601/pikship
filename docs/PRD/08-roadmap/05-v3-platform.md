# v3 — Adjacencies & advanced

> Goal: extend the surface beyond core aggregation. Hyperlocal, B2B, ONDC, possibly first-party. Adjacencies become real revenue.

## What v3 adds

### Hyperlocal & same-day (Feature 23)
- Borzo, Porter, Dunzo for Business, Shadowfax Hyperlocal, Loadshare integrations.
- Service types: 60-min, 2h, same-day, scheduled.
- Live driver tracking with map.
- Hyperlocal-specific buyer experience.

### B2B / freight (Feature 24)
- Safexpress, V-Trans, Spoton, Gati Surface partners.
- Consignment object (multi-piece).
- E-way bill auto-generation.
- POD capture.
- LTL + FTL service types.

### ONDC channel
- Pikshipp as ONDC seller-app participant.
- Inbound order ingestion via ONDC protocol.

### Pikshipp Express (first-party pilot — *if* metrics support)
- First-party last-mile in 2-3 metros (Mumbai, Bengaluru, Delhi).
- Plugged into our own aggregator as just another carrier adapter — same interface.
- Driver app (separate codebase).
- Hub operations + routing.
- This is a discrete bet — not committed unless v2 metrics support.

### International outbound (limited)
- FedEx, DHL, Aramex, ECMS, India Post International.
- HS codes; customs declarations.
- Restricted to specific seller cohort.

### Enterprise readiness
- SOC 2 Type II.
- Dedicated infrastructure for top-tier customers.
- SAML / OIDC SSO.
- Custom contracts and SLAs.
- Multi-region active-active.

### Adjacent products
- Working capital (advance against COD remittance) — partner-led.
- Returns-as-a-service (full QC + restock).
- Buyer-side checkout COD-confirm widget.

### Voice / IVR
- Buyer outreach for low-literacy via IVR.
- Multi-language voice prompts.

### Right-to-left language support
- Urdu, Arabic if international expansion.

## Strategic posture

By v3, the question is *not* "are we a credible aggregator?" but:
- Are adjacencies producing margin?
- Does Pikshipp Express prove the abstraction works for first-party — or expose flaws?
- Is the platform now a **default infrastructure choice** for Indian sellers?

## Risks

- First-party operational complexity.
- International compliance.
- Adjacent products distract from core.
- Enterprise drag changing product DNA.

## Long view

v3 is when Pikshipp's vision is either delivered or revealed insufficient. The PRD does not commit to v4 in advance — by then the strategy must be re-articulated against the realities the platform will have surfaced.
